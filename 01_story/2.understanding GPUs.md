# From RNN + Attention → Transformers 

## 1. Before Transformers: Attention + RNN

Before Transformers existed, **attention mechanisms were already used** — but not alone.

👉 They were **combined with RNNs**

- RNN handled the sequence (word-by-word processing)
- Attention helped the model **focus on important words**

---

## 2. The Big Idea 💡

Researchers proposed something bold:

> "What if we remove RNNs completely… and use only attention?"

This means:

- No sequential processing  
- No hidden-state chain  
- Full parallel computation   

 This became the **Transformer architecture**

---

## 3. Why Attention Is Powerful 

### 🔷 Problem with RNN

If **word 1** needs information from **word 50**:

- It must pass through **49 steps**
- Like a **telephone game**   
- Information gets weaker or distorted

---

### 🔷 Solution: Attention

Attention says:

> Every word can directly look at every other word

- Word 1 ↔ Word 50 = **direct connection**
- Distance doesn’t matter 

 This is called:

> **"Without regard to distance"**

---

## 4. Why This Changes Everything ⚡

- All words can be processed **at the same time**
- No waiting for previous steps
- Fully **parallelizable**

 Perfect for modern hardware like GPUs


# 5. What is a GPU?

A **GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)** is a special processor designed to:

- Perform **many calculations at once**
- Handle **massive parallel tasks**

---

###  CPU vs GPU (Easy Analogy)

- **CPU** = One smart worker doing tasks one by one  
- **GPU** = Thousands of small workers working together  

---

### 🔷 Why GPUs Matter for AI

Neural networks require:

- Huge matrix multiplications  
- Repeated calculations  

 GPUs can do these **much faster** than CPUs
